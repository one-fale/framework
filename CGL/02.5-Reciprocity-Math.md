CGL Part 2  introduced the equation behind the Reciprocity Ledger. This is where we slow down and show the working.

Some readers will want to see exactly how the numbers are built before they trust them. This document is for those readers. We take one example and run it through all three formulas, variable by variable, so the mechanics are fully visible.

The example is the Leifi History Project \- the genealogical reconstruction of the Leifi paramount title, validated through the Land and Titles Court in LC 10987 (2017). We use it because it is the one example in this series I can score with full authority. It is my own work, built over years, tested and proven in court. Nothing here is hypothetical.

## **A Principle From Customary Land**

Before we run the numbers, one idea needs to be in place, because it is the spine of everything that follows.

In Samoan customary land tenure, use does not equal ownership. A family can occupy land, work it, draw from it for generations \- but that use does not transfer title. Title belongs to the customary owners regardless of who is standing on the land at any given moment. And critically, use without consent or reciprocity is still a wrong, even where nothing has been physically removed. The land was never taken in the sense of being carried away. It was simply used, by someone with no right to use it, without anything given back.

This is the same principle the Reciprocity Ledger applies to data. When a system scrapes published knowledge \- genealogical records, court findings, family history \- and trains on it, nothing is physically stolen. The original documents still exist. The family still holds them. But the knowledge has been used, by a system with no consent to use it, and nothing has been returned. Under customary land logic, that is still extraction. The absence of physical theft does not make it reciprocal.

There is a second principle worth naming alongside this one, because it explains why the debt so rarely gets paid.

In Authors Guild v. Google, the courts eventually ruled in Google's favour on fair use grounds, a decade after the scanning began. By the time the ruling landed, the original act of extraction had become a closed, settled event \- something that happened, was litigated, and was over. Linear time did that work. It let the courts treat 2005 as sealed off from 2015, a finished chapter that could be ruled on once and never revisited.

The Samoa view of time will not allow that closure. If the past sits in front of us, it is never sealed off. It stays open to recalculation for as long as the relationship continues. A ruling does not end the conversation about whether a use was fair. It is simply one more entry in a ledger that keeps tracking the relationship after the case is closed.

That is the bridge this document is built to cross. The mathematics below measures land in the form of data: knowledge that was used without permission, exactly as land can be used without title \- and measured on a timeline that refuses to close.

## **The Three Instruments**

The Ledger runs on three calculations. Each one answers a different question.

**Sovereign Contribution Credits (SCC)** answer: what did the knowledge holder actually bring to this relationship? This is where the depth, the rarity, and the history of the knowledge gets measured.

**The Direct Asymmetry Index (DAI)** answers: how lopsided is the exchange? This compares what the knowledge was worth against what was actually returned.

**The Core Balancing Equation** answers: what is the net position? This is the final number \- the debt, made visible.

We will walk through all three using the Leifi History Project.

---

## **Part One: Sovereign Contribution Credits (SCC)**

SCC Value \= ((Rarity Index x Structural Depth) \+ (Cultural Depth x Verification Weight)) x Temporal Depth

Five variables. Each one is scored from 0 to 1\. A score close to 1 means the variable is at its strongest. A score close to 0 means it is weak or absent. The 0 to 1 range is a mathematical convention \- it keeps all five variables on the same scale so they can be combined consistently. The number itself only means something once it is anchored to something concrete, which is the job of the explainer attached to each variable below.

---

### **Rarity Index**

**The question it asks:** How rare is this knowledge? Could someone find it anywhere else, or does it live in only one place?

**Applied to the LHP:** The genealogical reconstruction draws on family records, church archives, oral history, and land documents spanning approximately one thousand years across both Samoas. No equivalent compiled record exists anywhere else. It is not duplicated in any public archive.

**Score: 0.9.** Close to the top of the scale because no substitute for this specific compiled record exists.

**How you would justify a different number:** If the same information were freely available in a published, widely accessible archive, the Rarity Index would drop, because the knowledge would no longer be scarce, even if it remained accurate.

---

### **Structural Depth**

**The question it asks:** How interconnected is this knowledge? Is it one isolated fact, or does it sit inside a larger system where everything depends on everything else?

**Applied to the LHP:** A single genealogical claim means little on its own. Its weight comes from how it connects to land records, title succession, church records, and oral tradition across generations \- each piece confirming and depending on the others. This is what the Rosetta Stone of Leone chapter demonstrates: branches separated by an ocean independently preserving convergent records.

**Score: 0.9.** High, because the knowledge is a connected system, not a standalone fact.

**How you would justify a different number:** A single isolated genealogical claim, with no corroborating structure around it, would score lower here, even if true.

---

### **Cultural Depth**

**The question it asks:** How central is this knowledge to the identity and self-understanding of the community that holds it?

**Applied to the LHP:** The Leifi title is not a record of historical curiosity. It carries land rights, status, and obligation within the aiga today. The reconstruction was undertaken at the direct request of the current titleholder, my father, to protect something the family understands as central to who they are.

**Score: 1.0.** Maximum, because there is no more central a marker of identity available to this family.

**How you would justify a different number:** Knowledge that is interesting but not identity-bearing \- a minor historical detail with no bearing on present-day standing \- would score lower here.

---

### **Verification Weight**

**The question it asks:** How well is this knowledge corroborated? Is it confirmed by multiple independent sources, or does it rest on a single uncorroborated claim?

**Applied to the LHP:** This is the variable with the strongest possible answer, because it has already been tested. The Land and Titles Court ruled on this evidence in LC 9946 (2015), LC 10987 (2017), and LC 12826 (2024). The Supreme Court granted an interim injunction recognising the same body of work. Independent adverse-source corroboration \- including church records confirming the 1935 transfer of the title, sourced from a separate family with no stake in the outcome \- has confirmed key findings.

**Score: 1.0.** Maximum. This is not self-reported knowledge. It has been independently verified, repeatedly, by a court of law and by sources outside the family.

**How you would justify a different number:** An uncorroborated oral claim, with no court finding and no independent confirmation, would score significantly lower \- not because it is untrue, but because it has not yet been tested.

---

### **Temporal Depth**

**The question it asks:** How long has this knowledge been held? Is this something recent, or has it been carried across generations?

**Applied to the LHP:** The genealogical reconstruction spans approximately one thousand years. The title itself has passed through dozens of holders across that span.

**Score: 0.95.** Very high, reflecting an exceptionally long chain of transmission and record.

**How you would justify a different number:** Knowledge that emerged recently \- even if accurate and significant \- would score lower here simply because it has not yet accumulated that depth of time.

---

### **Calculating the SCC**

SCC \= ((0.9 x 0.9) \+ (1.0 x 1.0)) x 0.95

SCC \= (0.81 \+ 1.00) x 0.95

SCC \= 1.81 x 0.95

SCC \= 1.72

**What this number means:** The Leifi History Project scores 1.72 on the SCC scale. This is the Sovereign Substrate \- the full weight of what was contributed, built over years of research and tested through multiple court findings. It is the number that gets ignored every time a system trains on published genealogical or legal material without consultation, credit, or return.

## **Part Two: The Direct Asymmetry Index (DAI)**

DAI \= Human Input Core Value (HIC) / AI Response Utility (ARU)

This formula compares two things: how essential the human contribution was, against how much value actually flowed back to the person who provided it.

**Human Input Core Value (HIC)** asks: how essential was this knowledge to the output? If you removed it, would the result still work? Court findings, published genealogical research, and legal reasoning of this kind cannot be generated from nothing. A system producing an answer about Samoan customary title succession is drawing on exactly this kind of compiled, verified record.

**Score: 1.0.** The contribution is fully essential. There is no substitute source for this specific, verified body of work.

**AI Response Utility (ARU)** asks: how much value, recognition, or return actually flowed back to the person who did the work? Did they receive payment, credit, consultation, or any form of benefit when that work was used?

**Applied to the LHP:** If this material were scraped and absorbed into a training set without consultation, the answer is nothing. No payment, no credit, no consultation, no acknowledgement that years of research and court testimony sit behind the answer a system produces.

**Score: 0.1.** Near zero, reflecting that almost no value would return under that scenario.

DAI \= 1.0 / 0.1

DAI \= 10

**What this number means:** Under this scenario, the system would be extracting ten times more value than it returns. This is not a marginal imbalance. It is the asymmetry made undeniable, using a published, court-tested body of work as the measure.

---

## **Part Three: The Core Balancing Equation**

Ledger Balance \= Input (Sovereign Substrate) \- Output (Computational Scale)

This is the simplest of the three calculations. It takes the SCC value \- what was contributed \- and subtracts the actual return that flowed back.

**Input (Sovereign Substrate):** 1.72 \- the SCC value calculated above.

**Output (Computational Scale):** 0.1 \- the ARU value, representing what would actually be returned under the extraction scenario.

Ledger Balance \= 1.72 \- 0.1

Ledger Balance \= 1.62

**What this number means:** The Ledger Balance is strongly positive. The contribution significantly exceeds the return. The debt is not abstract. It is 1.62 on a scale where zero would represent a fair exchange.

---

## **What the Ledger Does With This Number**

The Ledger does not stop once the calculation is run. It is a living instrument, not a one-time audit.

If this material is used in a commercial system, the Ledger requires consent verification before that use is considered legitimate. If that system generates revenue from outputs drawing on this knowledge, the DAI is recalculated to reflect the new scale of extraction. If the material is cited again, in a new product or a new context, a new calculation runs.

This is the same logic as customary land. Ongoing use requires an ongoing relationship with the title holder. It is not settled once and forgotten.

And it is the same logic that a fair use ruling cannot provide. A court can close a case. It cannot close a relationship. The Ledger stays open for as long as the knowledge is in use \- not because the law failed, but because the law was never built to track a relationship across time. It was built to settle a dispute and move on. The Ledger does the opposite. It does not move on.

## **Quick Glossary**

**Sovereign Contribution Credits (SCC)** \- The measure of what a knowledge holder brings to a relationship involving their knowledge. Built from five variables: Rarity Index, Structural Depth, Cultural Depth, Verification Weight, and Temporal Depth.

**Rarity Index** \- How scarce the knowledge is. High score means it exists in very few places.

**Structural Depth** \- How interconnected the knowledge is with a larger system. High score means it cannot be understood in isolation.

**Cultural Depth** \- How central the knowledge is to identity. High score means it is foundational, not incidental.

**Verification Weight** \- How well the knowledge is corroborated by independent sources. High score means multiple confirmations exist, including from sources with no stake in the outcome.

**Temporal Depth** \- How long the knowledge has been held and transmitted. High score means it spans many generations.

**Direct Asymmetry Index (DAI)** \- The ratio between how essential the human contribution was and how much value actually returned. A high number means a large imbalance.

**Human Input Core Value (HIC)** \- How essential the human contribution was to the final output. High score means the output could not exist without it.

**AI Response Utility (ARU)** \- How much value, credit, or benefit actually returned. Low score means little or nothing came back.

**Core Balancing Equation** \- The final calculation. Sovereign Substrate (SCC) minus Computational Scale (ARU). The result is the Ledger Balance \- the visible, measurable debt.

**Ledger Balance** \- The net position of the relationship at any given moment. Positive means the contributor is owed. The Ledger recalculates this continuously as use continues, rather than settling it once and closing the book.

## **The Short Version**

The Reciprocity Ledger measures what knowledge is actually worth \- using rarity, depth, cultural centrality, verification, and time held \- and compares that against what was actually received in return. The gap between those two numbers is the debt.

Use does not equal ownership. That is true of land, and it is true of data. The Ledger exists to make sure that when knowledge is used, the people who hold it are not left with nothing \- the way customary owners are never left with nothing simply because someone else worked their land.

That is the whole idea. Everything else is the mechanism for proving it.
